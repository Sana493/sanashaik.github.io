Why Data Science in Social and Interdisciplinary Contexts Matters

By Sana Shaik

← Back to Home

Data science is often seen as a purely technical field, but working with data about people makes it deeply social. Modeling social topics helps uncover patterns related to behavior, health, and inequality that are not always visible at the individual level.

Using data about people also comes with responsibility. Issues such as privacy, bias, consent, and fairness must be considered when analyzing social data. Poorly designed models can reinforce existing inequalities rather than reduce them.

Through this course, I hope to better understand how data science can be applied ethically, especially in interdisciplinary and healthcare-related contexts. I want to learn how to balance technical skill with social responsibility.
