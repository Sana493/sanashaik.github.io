Why Data Science in Social Contexts Matters

By Sana Shaik

Data science is often seen as a technical field — algorithms, models, and code. But the truth is, data science is deeply human. Every dataset represents real people, real decisions, and real consequences. When we ignore the social context behind data, we risk building systems that are technically correct but ethically flawed.

Understanding the social context helps us ask better questions, interpret results more carefully, and design solutions that actually help people rather than harm them. Data science becomes more meaningful when we connect it to lived experiences, inequalities, and the systems that shape people’s opportunities.

This reflection explores why context matters — and why responsible data science requires more than just technical skill.
