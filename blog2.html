Ethically Ambiguous Data Modeling

By Sana Shaik

Data models can be technically correct and still ethically questionable. A model may achieve high accuracy while reinforcing biases, excluding vulnerable groups, or making decisions that lack transparency.

Ethical ambiguity arises when we treat data as neutral — when in reality, data reflects the systems, inequalities, and assumptions that produced it. This blog explores how models can unintentionally cause harm and why ethical reflection must be part of every data science workflow.
